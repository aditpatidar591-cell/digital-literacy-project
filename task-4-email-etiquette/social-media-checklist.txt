Things you should do:

1. You should use secure passwords for everything you do online with passwords.

2. Take a moment to think before you post something on the internet.

3. Always respect others. Communicate with them in a polite way when you are online with social media and the internet.

4. Keep your information private and do not share it with anyone you do not know.

5. Verify the information you find online before you share it with your friends or family.

Things you should not do:

1. Do not share personal details about yourself with anyone you meet online.

2. Be careful. Do not click on links that look suspicious or weird.

3. Avoid posting things that're offensive or harmful to others when you are online.

4. Do not engage in cyberbullying because it is very mean and hurtful to others.

5. Do not trust people you do not know online easily because they may not be who they say they are and that can be bad, for you and your online security.

A common example of poor digital communication is sending unclear or rude emails without proper tone or structure. For instance, a student once sent a message to a professor saying, “Send marks fast,” without any greeting or respect. This created a negative impression and the professor ignored the message.

This situation could have been handled better by using proper email etiquette, such as including a greeting, writing politely, and clearly explaining the request. A respectful message like “Sir/Ma’am, I kindly request you to share my marks when possible” would have been more appropriate. This shows how important professional communication is in digital platforms.
